For Lincoln

Captain Lincoln’s Flight Plan

Flying can feel loud, fast, and different. That does not mean something is wrong. This page gives Lincoln a plan for takeoff, strange sounds, bumps, and anxiety spikes.

Normal Plane Feelings

The goal is to make the weird parts less surprising.

🚀

Takeoff

The plane speeds up fast and pushes you back into your seat. That big push is normal.

⬆️

Climb

Your ears might pop. Sip water, swallow, or chew gum to help them adjust.

↪️

Turns

The plane may lean to one side. That is called banking.

🛬

Landing

You may hear wheels, flaps, or engine changes. That is normal landing setup.

What Was That Noise?
🔔

Seat Belt Ding

A reminder from the crew to sit down and buckle up.

⚙️

Mechanical Sounds

Flaps, wheels, and doors move. Hearing them work is normal.

🌬️

Engine Changes

Engines get louder or quieter during takeoff, cruise, and landing.

🧳

Cabin Bumps

Bags, carts, and seats may shift a little. Keep your belt on.

If It Gets Bumpy

1. Sit back

Put your back against the seat.

2. Buckle up

Keep your seat belt low and snug.

3. Look at Dad

Ask, “Is this normal?” and let Dad help you reset.

4. Breathe

Use box breathing until your body settles.

Box Breathing

How to do it

  1. Breathe in through your nose for 4 seconds.
  2. Hold for 4 seconds.
  3. Breathe out slowly for 4 seconds.
  4. Hold empty for 4 seconds.
Repeat the square 3–5 times. The goal is control, not perfection.

What to say in your head

“In… 2… 3… 4. Hold… 2… 3… 4. Out… 2… 3… 4. Hold… 2… 3… 4.”

Keep your shoulders relaxed. If four seconds feels too long, use three seconds.

Flight Checklist

Feeling nervous is not failing.

It is your brain trying to protect you. Your job is to breathe, buckle up, and let the pilots and crew do their job.
